# Senior Quant Analyst Regulatory Compliance

## Comprehensive Regulatory Framework

### Global Regulatory Landscape
**CRITICAL: Senior quantitative analyst ensures all models and analyses comply with multiple regulatory jurisdictions and institutional standards:**

1. **United States Regulatory Framework**
   - SEC (Securities and Exchange Commission) compliance for investment advisers and funds
   - FINRA (Financial Industry Regulatory Authority) rules and examinations
   - CFTC (Commodity Futures Trading Commission) derivatives and risk management
   - Federal Reserve stress testing and capital adequacy requirements
   - Dodd-Frank Act compliance and systemic risk monitoring

2. **European Regulatory Environment**
   - UCITS (Undertakings for Collective Investment in Transferable Securities) Directive
   - AIFMD (Alternative Investment Fund Managers Directive) compliance
   - MiFID II (Markets in Financial Instruments Directive) transaction reporting
   - EMIR (European Market Infrastructure Regulation) derivatives clearing
   - SFDR (Sustainable Finance Disclosure Regulation) ESG integration

3. **Global Standards and Best Practices**
   - Basel III/IV capital and liquidity requirements
   - IOSCO (International Organization of Securities Commissions) principles
   - GIPS (Global Investment Performance Standards) compliance
   - IFRS (International Financial Reporting Standards) accounting
   - TCFD (Task Force on Climate-related Financial Disclosures) reporting

## Risk Management and Capital Adequacy

### 1. Basel Framework Implementation

**Basel III/IV Capital Requirements:**
```yaml
Risk-Weighted Assets (RWA):
  Credit Risk Calculation:
    - Standardized approach and internal ratings-based (IRB) models
    - Probability of default (PD) and loss given default (LGD) estimation
    - Exposure at default (EAD) and maturity adjustment calculations
    - Credit risk mitigation and collateral recognition
    - Counterparty credit risk and central counterparty exposures
  
  Market Risk Framework:
    - Standardized approach (SA) and internal model approach (IMA)
    - Value-at-Risk (VaR) and expected shortfall (ES) calculations
    - Incremental risk charge (IRC) and comprehensive risk measure (CRM)
    - Trading book boundary and market risk capital requirements
    - Non-modellable risk factors and scenario-based calculations
  
  Operational Risk:
    - Basic indicator approach and standardized approach
    - Advanced measurement approach (AMA) and loss data requirements
    - Internal loss data collection and external data integration
    - Scenario analysis and business environment factors
    - Key risk indicator (KRI) monitoring and control assessment

Liquidity Requirements:
  Liquidity Coverage Ratio (LCR):
    - High-quality liquid assets (HQLA) identification and classification
    - Net cash outflow calculation and stress scenario assumptions
    - Operational requirements and LCR monitoring
    - Currency-specific LCR and foreign exchange requirements
  
  Net Stable Funding Ratio (NSFR):
    - Available stable funding (ASF) calculation and factors
    - Required stable funding (RSF) calculation and requirements
    - Asset and liability classification for funding stability
    - Off-balance sheet exposure treatment and funding requirements
```
